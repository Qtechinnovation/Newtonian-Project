Newton's 1st Law

Inertia

Inertia is the law that states that an object in motion tends to remain in motion unless acted on by another force. The same applies for stationary objects. In the example above, the ball is an object at rest, and the other ball is a force that stops the ball from being at rest.

Newton's 2nd Law

Force

Newton's second law states that net force can be calculated by multiplying mass by acceleration.

Newton's 3rd Law

Reaction

Newton's third law states that every action has an equal and opposite reaction.
